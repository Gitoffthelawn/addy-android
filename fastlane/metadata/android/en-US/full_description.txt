<p>Manage your AnonAddy aliases, recipients, domains and more with the AnonAddy for Android app.</p>
<p><strong>Requirements:<br /></strong>AnonAddy instance running v0.8.1 or higher</p>
<p><strong>Characteristics</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Connect to the AnonAddy instance or your own hosted instance</li>
<li>A unique, sexy and sleek design, based on the Material You Design guidelines</li>
<li>Support for Deep Link to easily deactivate aliases from the email banner</li>
<li>Lock the app using Biometrics</li>
<li>App exclusive features
<ul>
<li>Watching aliases for new emails</li>
<li>Get notified when a new update is available</li>
<li>Send emails from aliases by selecting an email address or clicking a mailto link</li>
<li>Get notified on failed deliveries</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Enable error logging to *locally* store exceptions for easy troubleshooting</li>
<li>Widgets!</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Security:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Encrypted preferences, your API key and other AnonAddy related settings are securely stored on your device using the AndroidX crypto library</li>
<li>No stats, buried points or Device IDs, or even crash reporting (Except for Google Play Store builds). So if you get a crash, please share the crash info with me &gt;_&lt;.</li>
<li>I am forgoing the convenient third-party collection SDK and various stats just so you can use it with confidence.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Manage (add, edit, delete):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Aliases</li>
<li>Recipients</li>
<li>Domains</li>
<li>Usernames</li>
<li>Rules</li>
<li>Failed deliveries</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What is AnonAddy</strong></p>
<p>AnonAddy is an open source email forwarding service designed to protect an Internet user's email addresses from spam and other unwanted emails.
